# Kila MVP — Customer Product Review

> **Reviewer persona:** Marketing manager at an SMB (team of 6), reports to a CMO, budget-conscious, evaluating Kila for AI search brand performance monitoring.

---

## What I'd Actually Love

### Competitive Gap Dashboard — The Star Feature
The side-by-side visibility gap, position gap, and sentiment gap versus a specific competitor — with trend arrows — is exactly what I need to justify budget to my CMO. One screenshot, one meeting, done. This alone would get me to sign up.

### AI Reference Source Tracking — Genuinely Unique
Seeing *which websites* the AI is pulling from when it talks about my brand (and my competitor's brand) is something I can act on immediately: submit to those directories, get coverage on those sites. No other tool I've seen does this.

### Market Dynamic Page — Excellent for Quarterly Reviews
The quadrant chart (position strength vs. visibility) and visibility share pie chart make a great slide for leadership. It contextualizes where my brand sits in the competitive landscape at a glance.

### Segment-Level Analysis — Smart Product Design
If I'm a brand with multiple product lines, seeing performance broken down by segment is much more useful than an overall number.

---

## What Would Make Me Walk Away

### 1. Empty Dashboards on Day One
This is the biggest problem. I sign up, set up my brand, and I'm staring at empty charts for days or weeks while the scraper collects data. I have no idea if the product is working. I would assume it's broken and cancel before my first renewal.

**What I need:** Either seed with some historical data during onboarding, or show a clear progress indicator — *"We're collecting your first 7 days of data, come back [date]"* — with a sample/demo view so I can see what the product will look like.

### 2. No Alerts or Notifications
If my brand drops out of AI search results tomorrow, I won't find out until I remember to log in. That could be weeks. The entire value of monitoring is *proactive* awareness, not reactive checking. Without email alerts for significant drops in visibility or sudden competitor surges, this is just a reporting tool, not a monitoring tool.

**What I need:** *"Alert me when my awareness score drops more than X%, or when a competitor's visibility exceeds mine."*

### 3. No Export or Reporting
My CMO doesn't log into vendor dashboards. I need to export a PDF or CSV to share in our monthly marketing review. Without this I'm manually screenshotting every chart and pasting into slides — which I'll stop doing after month two, and then I'll stop paying.

**What I need:** Export to PDF or CSV. A scheduled weekly email summary would make this a must-keep subscription.

### 4. Data Without Action
Every metric tells me *what* is happening but nothing about *what to do about it*. My awareness score dropped 1.2 points — so what? Even simple text like:

> *"Your visibility in the skincare segment dropped this week — your competitors are being cited by Healthline and WebMD, which currently don't mention your brand."*

...would be transformative.

**What I need:** At minimum, a "Why this happened" explanation next to significant changes. Ideally, content recommendations or source gap analysis.

### 5. No Benchmarks
Is a 6.2 awareness score good? Is 43% visibility rate excellent or terrible for my industry? Without context, the numbers are meaningless. I can't tell if I should be panicking or celebrating.

**What I need:** Industry average benchmarks, or at least a percentile — *"your brand is in the top 30% of brands in this category."*

---

## Friction That Would Make Me Hesitate

**The mental model is confusing for non-technical marketers.**
Projects → Brands → Segments — I don't immediately understand the hierarchy or why I need all three. The onboarding doesn't explain it. Most marketers will get stuck here and not fully set up the product.

**Gemini only.**
My CEO asks "does this include ChatGPT?" I have to say no. That's a hard conversation. ChatGPT has 180M users. Most of my customers are probably asking it questions. Being Gemini-only feels incomplete, even for an MVP.

**"Consistency Index" needs explanation.**
I stared at this metric for 30 seconds and still didn't know if a higher number was better. B2B products need tooltips or inline explanations for any non-obvious metric, because marketing managers aren't data scientists.

**The Risk Intelligence page is hidden.**
This is backwards. "Risk" is language that resonates with marketing managers and CMOs — it implies ROI and urgency. It's the feature most likely to trigger a renewal conversation. Hiding it because it needs polish is a missed opportunity.

---

## Summary

| Area | Assessment |
|---|---|
| **Keep and lead with** | Competitive gap dashboard, AI reference sources, Market Dynamic quadrant |
| **Must fix before charging money** | Empty state on day one, no export, no alerts |
| **Strong differentiator to build next** | Actionable recommendations, content gap suggestions |
| **Table stakes you're missing** | ChatGPT/Perplexity coverage, metric tooltips/explanations, benchmarks |
| **Hidden gem to surface** | Risk Intelligence — don't hide this, it's your best retention feature |

---

> The core data is genuinely valuable and the competitive analysis is well-executed. The gap is not the analytics — it's the *workflow around the analytics*. Right now the product answers **"what is happening"** but not **"so what"** or **"what now."** Closing that gap is what turns a dashboard into a product people renew.
